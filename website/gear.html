Suggested gear

Though not enforced, most all trails require riders to wear helmets. Be sure to swing by your local bike shop to pick up a stylish, certified helmet!
Let's face it, flats are inevitable on the rugged off road course. Be prepared with a spare tube to keeping you rolling!
What use is a spare tube without a pump? A compact pump such as the one pictured fits snug on the bicycle and if (and when)you have a flat, or just experience a loss of air, you can be prepared!
Everyone thinks riding a bicycle is simple, but let's face it, off road mountain biking features steep inclines and drops, with scattered roots, rocks, and even critters on the trail. Prepare for the worst with a basic compact first aid kit. Suggested materials are: alcohol sanitizer, bandages and wraps, and snake venom medication.
Always remember to stay hydrated while on the trail! Mountain biking is a strenuous activity and much water can be excreted from the body in the form of sweat. Always take plenty of water with you when you ride, atleast two liters, and maybe more if you choose to make the ride an all day event. A camel back such as the one pictured is handy as it allows the rider to maintain their hydration, without slowing down the pace and also allows for storage of tools and snacks.
